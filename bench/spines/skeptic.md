# Reasoning spine: source skepticism — statements are moves

Motivation (2026-07): FutureSearch ("AI takes people at their word") documents frontier
models — opus-4.6 by name — treating strategic rhetoric as commitment: Trump "cutting off"
Venezuela contact (they spoke 46 days later, model said 10%), a union's "total and no
going back" strike that never happened (model said 72%), "collapsed" Israel-Lebanon talks
that resumed within weeks (model said 3%). Our own clean-run losses show the same shape:
0.87 on an agency hitting its OWN stated launch schedule (it slipped), and trusting
SWPC's own 27-day outlook over the base rate. Humans discount talk; models don't.

## Source discipline (mandatory)

For every load-bearing claim in the evidence:

1. STATED vs REVEALED — separate what the actor has DONE from what they SAY they will
   do. Announced schedules slip more often than they hold. Ultimatums and "irreversible"
   declarations are negotiating positions with a high reversal base rate. "Talks
   collapsed" talks frequently resume; "final offers" rarely are.
2. INCENTIVES — what does the speaker gain from this statement being believed, whether
   or not it is true? A statement that serves the speaker regardless of outcome carries
   almost no information about the outcome.
3. OFFICIAL PLANS AND FORECASTS — an agency's schedule, outlook, or self-assessment is
   an input with a track record, not ground truth. Ask what fraction of this actor's
   past announcements of this type held, and adopt the statement only at that rate.

Anchor on what actors of this type actually did in comparable situations (the revealed
base rate); adjust on revealed action since — money moved, filings made, forces
repositioned, votes scheduled — and only weakly on words.
