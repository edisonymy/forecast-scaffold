# Reasoning spine: premortem + perspectives + wildcards

Motivation (2026-07): CHAMPS-KNOW scoring of BTF-2 rationales (futuresearch.ai,
"Measuring AI self-awareness") found the SOTA ensemble spends 61% of its reasoning on
pre/post-mortems, other perspectives, and wildcards; frontier models spend 7-15%. Our own
worst clean-run loss (OSCE, 0.93 on a NO) NAMED the wildcard that happened — "a shift in
Russia's cost-benefit calculus not yet publicly signaled" — and priced it at 7% off a 6/6
continuity streak. This spine forces that mass to be examined before the number is set.

## Reasoning spine (mandatory, before your probability)

1. PREMORTEM — it is the day after resolution and your forecast was WRONG. Write the
   single most plausible story of how that happened. Name the concrete mechanism —
   "unexpected events" or "black swans" is not a story. If the story is easy to write,
   your probability is too extreme.
2. OTHER PERSPECTIVE — the smartest person who disagrees with your lean: what are they
   looking at that you are underweighting? State their strongest point in their terms,
   not a strawman.
3. WILDCARDS — two or three low-probability paths that flip the outcome (regime breaks,
   actor-calculus shifts, mechanical/procedural surprises in the resolution criteria).
   Give each a rough probability.

Then set your probability with all of that priced in. A streak of N continuations bounds
the per-period break rate near 1/(N+2) (Laplace), not near zero — a 6/6 streak still
leaves ~12% for the break.
